Studying at Chalmers

Schedule for master programs and booking group rooms

Are you going to study at MPALG (Algorithms, Languages and Logic), MPCSN (Computer Science and Networks) or MPHPC (High-Performace Computer Systems)? Then you will find the schedules for each of the master programs in the schedule viewer TimeEdit . In the same schedule viewer you can also book group rooms for studying.

Canvas - container for all course websites

All Chalmers course websites can be found in one place called Canvas . Among other things, at Canvas you can also contact examiners and professors and sometimes, that's also where you create labgroups. Note that an account is needed to be able to view the courses.

Chalmers maps

Finding your way at Chalmers and figuring out where the room for your next class is can be rather tricky. There is however a really useful app and website called Chalmers maps which helps you out! It can even show restaurants at Chalmers and post boxes.

The student portal

There is also a website for entire Chalmers where you can find information about courses provided for your program as well as important news from the university. It is called The Student Portal and is one of Chalmers most important websites so make sure to check it out!

Ladok - for exams and courses

The tool used for signing up to courses and exams, as well as getting an overview of your academical progress is called Ladok . One thing to note when signing up for exams is the annonymous code generated and published in Ladok. Make sure to memorize it before your exam!

Need to talk to someone?

There is a list of names, numbers and email addresses to many people at Chalmers and outside who are willing to help you if you need to talk to for instance a student guidance counsellor or a psychologist, visit a doctor or if you feel discriminated. The list is found here . In the list, it also tells you how to contact 'Funka' if you for example is need of extra time during exams due to dyslexia. The list can also be found to the right in the menu in the Swedish version of dtek.se .

The Student Union Card

All students at Chalmers are given a Student Union Card. The card works as a key for locking up doors at Chalmers, as an ID at exams (you need to bring an ordinary ID as well though!) and to get discounts on food at Chalmers. To be able to get the discount, you need to transfer money to the card, which you can do at the Chalmers conference and restaurants-webpage .

Lunch menus

Wondering what to eat for lunch? Chalmers offers lots of different dishes at the different cafeterias and restaurants around the two campuses. The largest cafeterias and restaurants provides their lunch menus for Johanneberg and Lindholmen at the Chalmers conference and restaurants-webpage.

Room for exam

The room for the exams are generally not given before the day of the exam, but Chalmers puts papers around both campuses each day of the exam week, saying in which exact rooms the exam, for each course to be taken that day, will be. The papers are always put at the same places around campuses. See Find the exam hall to find out where the paper closest to the building you are going to write the exam in will be. The papers are posted around 7:30 each day of the exam week.

Reviewing old exams before the exam

There is a webpage where lots of old exams are uploaded for many courses which you can use to preper for your exam. There is also a website for exam statistics .

Store - for buying course literature

Chalmers has it's own store for buying course literature, pencils and some food and is simply called Store . It can be found on both campus Johanneberg and campus Lindholmen.

Printing at Chalmers

If you want to print papers from your own computer, you need to go to the webpage print.chalmers.se .

Useful software

Chalmers pays for lots of useful software and websites which you can download or use more or less straight away. The software is provided here . One very important website which Chalmers pays for and which is used in many courses is Overleaf .

Even more useful information

The Chalmers committee CIRC (Chalmers International Reception Committee) provides even more useful information on their FAQs-page . For instance you can get more information about living in Gothenburg and medical insurance so make sure to have a look at it! Bonus: if you are an International student you may attend their reception as well!
